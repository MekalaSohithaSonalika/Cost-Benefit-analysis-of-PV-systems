GRID-TIED PV SYSTEM

A grid-tied photovoltaic (PV) system is a solar power setup that is connected directly to the public electricity grid. It allows the solar panels to generate electricity and supply power to a home or business while also sending excess energy back to the grid. When solar energy production is insufficient, the system draws power from the grid. This type of system is popular because it eliminates the need for battery storage, reduces electricity bills, and often qualifies for net metering, where users receive credit for the surplus energy they contribute to the grid.

DUAL-MODE PV SYSTEM

A dual-mode photovoltaic (PV) system is a solar power system that can operate both in grid-tied and off-grid modes. It provides flexibility by allowing the system to supply power directly to the grid when it's connected, or switch to off-grid mode during grid outages or in remote locations. These systems typically include battery storage to store excess solar energy for use when the grid is unavailable. Dual-mode PV systems offer the benefits of continuous power supply, energy independence, and backup power during emergencies, making them a versatile solution for various energy needs.
